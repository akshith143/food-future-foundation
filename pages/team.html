DR Chandrakant S Pandav

Dr Chandrakant S Pandav is the President of the Indian Coalition for Control of Iodine Deficiency Disorders. Formerly Head of Public Health and Community at AIIMS, New Delhi, he has been a relentless crusader in the fight for sustainable elimination of Iodine Deficiency Disorders (IDD) - the single most important cause of mental handicap globally. His research and advocacy have contributed enormously to the success of the National Iodine Deficiency Disorders Control Programme (NIDDCP) and the mandatory Universal Salt Iodization (USI) in India. In 2021, he has been conferred Padma Shri for his exceptional and distinguished service in the field of medicine.

He obtained both his MBBS and MD in Community Medicine from AIIMS, New Delhi. Later, he went on to study at the Department of Human Nutrition at the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine. He also obtained Master’s in Science from McMaster University, Hamilton, Canada with specialization in Health Economics, Clinical Epidemiology and Biostatistics in 1991.

He has served on various committees and expert groups set up by ICMR, FSSAI Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, Ministry of Women and Child Development, and NITI Aayog, Government of India. As a member of the National Council on India Nutrition Challenges under POSHAN Abhiyan, he is advising the district administration in aspirational districts in the country.

He is prolific researcher and writer co-editing 14 books, 250 research papers, 100 research abstracts, 100 technical reports/monographs and 50 books (edited books and chapters). With over 5000 citations, h-index 32 and i10-index 92, his research papers are widely used. He is also reviewer of various reputed national and international journals. He is also attached to various foundations, such as the Satsang Foundation and Manav Ekta Mission from 2012 (as General Council Member) and Sri Satya Sai Health and Education Trust, Bangalore (as member of the Advisory Board).

DR Rajan Sankar

Dr Rajan Sankar is Senior Advisor, Nutrition at Tata Trusts. He was Founding Director of The India Nutrition Initiative (TINI), an initiative of Tata Trusts. He has served in the Indian Armed Forces Medical Services for 24 years. At the time of retirement, he was Senior Advisor in Medicine and Head of department of Endocrinology at the Institute of Nuclear Medicine and Allied Sciences (INMAS) at New Delhi.

Over the past two decades, Dr. Sankar has devoted more time to public health nutrition with a focus on equitable scale-up of proven high-impact nutrition interventions. He has established several nutrition initiatives in India and Bangladesh and has directed several large-scale programs for the elimination of iodine deficiency disorders, other micronutrient deficiencies, and improving maternal, infant and young child nutrition. He has worked extensively with governments in India and many countries in South and South-east Asia. He has helped build multi-sectoral alliances of governments, businesses, and civil society organizations in India and Bangladesh to help transform the environment for nutrition programming. Prior to joining the Trusts, Dr. Sankar was country manager, India and Regional Representative, South Asia, Global Alliance for Improved Nutrition (GAIN) and also worked for UNICEF in India.

DR Ishi Khosla

Dr. Ishi Khosla is a practicing clinical nutritionist, columnist, author, an entrepreneur, researcher and welfare worker. She is actively involved in clinical practice at the Centre for Dietary Counselling in Delhi where she deals with a wide range of nutrition related health problems including obesity, diabetes, cardio-vascular disease, food allergies, digestive, immune system, neuro psychiatric and endocrine disorders in adults and children.

She is a Masters in Food and Nutrition from Lady Irwin College, Delhi University. She worked as a consultant in the Endocrinology Department at AIIMS and Sita Ram Bhartiya Institute, New Delhi and later as a Senior Clinical Nutritionist at the Escorts Heart Institute and Research Centre, New Delhi. In 2001, she established Centre for Dietary Counselling and started independent clinical practice and also founded ‘Whole Foods’, India’s first health food company. She also launched www.theweightmonitor.com, the first of its kind in India, a fully online weight management programme In May, 2006, she founded the Celiac Society of India, the first and only non-profit organisation has made it its mission to create awareness on early diagnosis and management of celiac disease.

She has been conferred several awards that include ‘‘Women’s Achiever’s award in the Field of Nutrition by The Imperial & Cancer Patient Aids Association and ‘Jijabai Women Achiever’s award’. She was also listed among 25 most powerful women in India by the India Today Group.

Ajay Bohora

Ajay Bohora is the founder of Credila, the first private students’ loan company in India later taken over by HDFC. Starting his career with Tata Exports, he worked with MetLife in New York and then started a healthcare-focused BPO that was acquired by the global BPO giant WNS Global Services. An engineering graduate from VJTI, Mumbai, he earned his MBA from Hoftra University, New York.

Ajay has served as a member of the committee on "Student Financial Aid" of Government of India, Delhi. He also has served as the member of the Senate of 125 years old prestigious engineering institute, VJTI, Mumbai. Apart from this, he has been a member of organizations like Higher Education Network, FICCI. He is a recipient of "Engineering Achievement Award" by The Institute of Engineers (India) for the year 2007-08 and the "Emerging Leader of the Year Award" in 2012-13. He was declared as “Promising Entrepreneurs of India” by the Economic Times.

He is passionate about education and public health. He co-founded New Era English School at Nasik. This school is rated as one of the most innovative schools in India.
