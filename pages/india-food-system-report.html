India Food System Vision 2030 Report

By 2030, we envision a world where hunger and all forms of malnutrition are eliminated by ensuring that everyone has access to safe, nutritious and affordable food. While, doing so, we must sustain biodiversity and prevent environmental degradation and save livelihoods. Sadly, we are not track. The number of hungry people is 690 million and rising; 1.4 billion people cannot afford a nutritionally adequate diet; the world is off-target to meet the Paris Agreement climate target of limiting global warming to a 1.5°C rise; biodiversity trends are negative; and poverty levels are on the rise again due to the pandemic. It is therefore essential to bend the above trends towards more positive outcome by transforming food systems. As the world’s most populous country with a population of 1.5 billion in 2030 (about one-fifth of the global population), India has to play an important role to change this trajectory. Hence, there is a need to transform our food systems.

Recognising that the transformation of the food system is the most powerful way to change the course and realise the vision of Sustainable Development Goals 2030 (SDG- 2030), the United Nations has convened the UN Food System Summit (UNFSS) as part of the Decade of Action to achieve the SDGs later this year. Details of the UN Food System Summit are available at  https://www.un.org/en/food-systems-summit.

In the above context, the Coalition of Partners has published the ‘India Food System Vision 2030 Report’. Recognizing that India commemorates 75 years of its independence, the report would focus on 75 Areas of Collective Action and Innovation (ACAIs). For each area, essays would be written by experts, bringing out developments since independence, the current state of play outlining concrete achievements and a bold vision for 2030 stated. The essays would outline the innovations required and the pathways to be taken to reach the proposed vision.

With actionable, concrete, scalable & motivating solutions, the vision 2030 report would excite stakeholders to act. The broad areas covering the food system have been categorized under 6 broad heads, namely –

  • Production - Nature positive food production; Sustainable livestock and fisheries
  • Processing - Food Processing; Logistics and Supply Chain
  • Plate - Food safety and hygiene; Nutrition and Immunity
  • Planet - Climate change; Food loss and Waste
  • People - Promote Livelihoods; Social Protection
  • Policy  - Regulation and governance; Funding and Investment
