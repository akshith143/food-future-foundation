Food Systems Leadership Program

OBJECTIVE

Food Systems Leaders Programme (FSLP) is aimed at creating a pool of young professionals working together to transform food system in India and beyond. Targeting early career professionals with diverse backgrounds in food and agriculture sector, the programme seeks to leverage energy, youth, and altruism of the young people. This would enable them to undertake a unique personal transformation and leadership journey and make a difference at national and global level. This would also provide high-impact, hands-on experiences to prepare them for meaningful and productive careers.

Given that the food systems are changing rapidly and highly interconnected -- the most and the best knowledge about "what to do" resides collectively with the people who work in various parts of the system. Each of them has different parts of the knowledge, a different framework, a different set of understandings, and different information from the network of communications and information. Each sees different pieces of the future. This programme would access the knowledge and insights not only in its collective form but in its synergistic form and help the participants to find solutions for the future that reside collectively in the food system.

As the food system grows further, becomes more complex, more interconnected, and more interdependent, we must be able to look at the whole system and understand its parts all at once. We must be able to ask “what-if” questions about possible future and be creative and courageous about system redesign. The Programme aims to enable the participants to see things, think about things and do things differently.

Overall, the Programme is designed to develop leadership capacity for food system transformation, provide strong action orientation through ability to design and implement action- oriented projects and develop a network of young leaders who would take the mantle of food system transformation in India and globally.

Eligibility & Selection

The program is open for early career professionals up to 35 years of age (as on 1 st July, 2021) working in the corporate, government & non-profit sector in food, agriculture & related areas can apply for the program. Applicants would be selected after taking into consideration the profile of the candidate, representation across different domains and gender and geographical representation.

Program Duration & Class Timings

3 months, with 3 hours of active engagement and an expectation of 3-6 hours of self-learning per week. Classes will be held from 1600 – 1730 hours on Fridays and 1100 – 1230 hours on Saturdays.

Program Structure

The program would combine classroom sessions, continuous learning and an enabling support system. Learning will be mainly through specially curated learning sessions with domain experts and leaders in the food system.

The learning pedagogy will be based on facilitating learning on the key pathways for transforming food systems.

The In-Class Component would be delivered over 3 modules:

Food Systems Insights

These sessions will be facilitated by experts and professionals and cover different elements of the food system covering the ‘Six Ps’– Production, Processing, Plate, Planet, People and Policy and their interdependencies. Sessions will be held every Friday. The faculty led talk will be followed by a group discussion and presentations on learning by the participants.

Food Systems Leaders Speak

These sessions are focused on building leadership skills and will be delivered by industry leaders, facilitated through fire-side chats. Sessions will be held alternate Saturdays. Learning from the sessions will reinforced through curated presentations form the participants on various leadership styles and tools discussed.

Food Systems Showcase

These sessions are design to build and widen knowledge on policies and best practices from across the globe in food systems in case study format. Sessions will be held alternate Saturdays, and will include a varied mix of speakers from coalition partners and global experts.

In addition, participants would be expected to spend about 4 to 6 hours in self-learning, peer-to- peer learning, assignments, and action-oriented projects. All the participants are obliged to timely submit completed assignments at the end of the course. Assignments will be based on on-going live projects aligned with the India Food Systems Vision. The programme will have special focus on systems thinking and design thinking, fostering teamwork and improving communication skills, bringing in multiple perspectives and ability to sense the future. Overall, the programme would enable changing mind-sets and honing systemic thinking skills in individuals in supporting the food system transformation.

Passing Criteria

Certificate of Completion shall be issued on acquiring 60% marks in the projects and continuous evaluation; and meeting 75% attendance.

FOOD SYSTEM TRANSFORMATION LEADERS NETWORK(FSTLN)

In order to foster an integrated approach to the design and operationalization of solutions for food system transformation, graduates of the Food System Transformation Leaders Programme would develop a Food System Transformation Leaders Network (FSTLN). This is envisaged as a multi-stakeholder platform for ‘Multi Stakeholder Collaboration for Systemic Change’ (MSCFSC). This Network will grow and expand over time. The Network’s overall goal is to build a committed group of leaders to help deliver on the 2030 Agenda leading up to and beyond the Summit.

The Network would to move the discussion from ‘what needs to change’ to mechanisms that need to be put in place to facilitate these changes. They would contribute to substantive discussions, generate ideas and act to strengthen food systems, and share information to ensure anyone with an interest in food systems at all levels can engage with the Summit.

The Network will help forming connections and building lasting relationships. As a bridge between sector professionals, corporates, and domain experts, FSTLN is committed to strengthening the spirit of connection and collaboration in India’s food system. This network would align its activities with the UN Food System Summit 2021 and engage with all stakeholders with central focus on the identification and operationalization of ‘game-changing solutions’.

Value Addition to Participants:

  • Recognition & Prestige on being India’s Chosen 35 Under 35 to the country’s most elite program for future leaders in Food Systems
  • Recognition of the Certificate of Completion by the Government of India, leading Corporates & Multinational agencies
  • Networking opportunities with eminent Faculty, Industry Leaders & the leaders of tomorrow in the food & agriculture space
  • Participation in Exciting Live Projects aimed at transforming India’s Food System Vision, while being mentored by experienced professionals in the food space
  • Access to case studies, lecture videos and more
  • Development of leadership skills
