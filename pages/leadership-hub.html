Food Systems Leadership Program

OBJECTIVE

The Food Systems Leaders Programme (FSLP) aims to bring together early career professionals with diverse backgrounds in food and agriculture sector. The programme seeks to leverage the energy, youth, and altruism of such professionals.

The 12 Week program works to create a pool of young professionals working who can work together to transform food system in India and beyond, thus enabling them to undertake a unique personal journey and make a difference at a greater level. This would also provide high-impact, hands-on experiences to prepare them for future challenges.

Overall, the Programme has been tailored to fit young professionals who have shown glimpses of remarkable innovation and dexterity to solve problems that plague society at large. These potential change makers will be given training and mentorship to develop into leaders with practical expertise in the subject matter. These young leaders would thus take the mantle of food system transformation in India and globally.

Key Value Additions from the programme:

  • Recognition & Prestige on being India’s Chosen 35 Under 35, to the country’s most elite program for future leaders in Food Systems
  • Recognition of the Certificate of Completion by the Government of India, leading Corporates & Multinational agencies
  • Networking opportunities with eminent faculty, industry leaders & the leaders of tomorrow in the food & agriculture space
  • Participation in Exciting Live Projects aimed at transforming India’s Food System Vision, while being mentored by experienced professionals in the food space
  • Access to case studies, lecture videos and more
  • Development of leadership skills

Eligibility & Selection

The Programme would be open to:

  • Early career professionals, upto 35 years of age (as on 1st July 2021)
  • Working in the corporate, government & non-profit sector in food, agriculture & related areas

Applicants would be selected after taking into consideration the profile of the candidate, representation across different domains and gender and geographical representation.

Program Duration & Class Timings

  • Total duration of the program is 12 weeks.
  • Entails 3 hours of active engagement and an expectation of 3-6 hours of self-learning per week.
  • Class timings: 1600 – 1730 hours on Fridays and 1100 – 1230 hours on Saturdays.

Program Structure

The program would combine classroom sessions, continuous learning and an enabling support system. Learning will be mainly through specially curated learning sessions with domain experts and leaders in the food system.

The learning pedagogy will be based on facilitating learning on the key pathways for transforming food systems.

The In-Class Component would be delivered over 3 modules:

Food Systems Insights

These sessions will be facilitated by experts and professionals and cover different elements of the food system covering the ‘Six Ps’– Production, Processing, Plate, Planet, People and Policy and their interdependencies. Sessions will be held every Friday. The faculty led talk will be followed by a group discussion and presentations on learning by the participants.

Food Systems Leaders Speak

These sessions are focused on building leadership skills and will be delivered by industry leaders, facilitated through fire-side chats. Sessions will be held alternate Saturdays. Learning from the sessions will reinforced through curated presentations form the participants on various leadership styles and tools discussed.

Food Systems Showcase

These sessions are design to build and widen knowledge on policies and best practices from across the globe in food systems in case study format. Sessions will be held alternate Saturdays, and will include a varied mix of speakers from coalition partners and global experts.

In addition, participants would be expected to spend about 4 to 6 hours in self-learning, peer-to-peer learning, assignments, and action-oriented projects. All the participants are obliged to timely submit completed assignments at the end of the course. Assignments will be based on on-going live projects aligned with the India Food Systems Vision. The programme will have special focus on systems thinking and design thinking, fostering teamwork and improving communication skills, bringing in multiple perspectives and ability to sense the future. Overall, the programme would enable changing mind-sets and honing systemic thinking skills in individuals in supporting the food system transformation.

Passing Criteria

Certificate of Completion shall be issued on acquiring 60% marks in the projects and continuous evaluation; and meeting 75% attendance.

FOOD SYSTEM TRANSFORMATION LEADERS NETWORK(FSTLN)

To foster an integrated approach to the design and operationalization of solutions for food system transformation, graduates of the Programme would create a Food System Transformation Leaders Network (FSTLN).

The Network will help forming connections and building lasting relationships. As a bridge between sector professionals, corporates, and domain experts, FSTLN is committed to strengthening the spirit of connection and collaboration in India’s food system.

This network would align its activities with the UN Food System Summit 2021 and engage with all stakeholders with central focus on the identification and operationalization of ‘game-changing solutions’.
